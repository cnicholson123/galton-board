Balls drain from the hopper at top, then bounce through a triangular peg lattice via real gravity + collisions. Tiny randomness on each peg strike (adjustable) models real-world imperfections — this is what produces the binomial spread, exactly as on a physical board. Bin counts are compared live against the theoretical Binomial(n = rows, p = 0.5) distribution. The hopper holds a finite supply — once it drains, press Reset to refill.
